Introduction

Project Overview

  • The website application I will be creating is for a student organization group at UNC Charlotte known as CLT Nayaki, an indian classical dance team. The website will serve as an informational site on the goals and purpose of the team.
  • The intended users of the website is for existing members of the team, new incoming members, and for new incoming dance memebers in the future.
  • Overall the content of the website will include information on who CLT Nayaki is, what they do, team introductions and their roles, the different types of dance forms, differnet events and performances that they and many more. The website content should encourange particpation and communication while also being able to view spread awareness of the team.

Page Design

Home Page

  1. Name of the page: Home
  2. Purpose of the page: To draw viewers to explore the webpage further and prompt them to dive deeper into the various pages offered on the website.
  3. Audience/Users of page: Current members of the CLT Nayaki Organization, students who want to know explore and know more about the organization and potential new students who want to join the team.
  4. Content: Summary of CLT Nayaki, photo gallery and contact information.
  5. This page will include a text box prompt for the viewer to enter data upon their wish to receive updates or any questions they had. One for name and one for email.
  6. The home page will include validations for the text box entries. For example, the entry cannot be a numner.
  7. The home page will include a button for the photo gallery and some hyperlinks but no drop down menu.
  8. In the home page, when the user clicks on the arrow mark button in the photo gallery the action will either move forward in the slideshow or back depending on the arrow mark. Additionally the hyperlinks will link to other links for the user to explore.
  9. There will be no special notes regarding the page.

  4. Content: Summary of CLT Nayaki, picture of each team executive board memeber and bios.
